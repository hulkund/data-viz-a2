Subtheme: Housing for the Rich

Overall Analysis Questions

This dataset contains statistics for all residential sales transactions in the City of Boston between 2000-2023. It has columns with information about sellers, buyers, price, location, whether a sale is a flip or a cash sale, or if the current owner is the buyer. Some data transformation has been done: I was mainly interested in looking at residential data so I filtered for condominiums and single family residences. I’m interested in this dataset because it has different dimensions for what makes a home a luxury/investment property: along the lines of pricing, investor buys, and specific areas in Boston.
  1. When did flipping begin in Boston? Is it a recent trend? How does flipping correlate to cash sales and current ownership of housing? In my own hometown of Seattle, the flip rates of housing have increased drastically over the years, pushing the value of most single family homes by over $500K in the last three years alone. I'm curious to see if these trends hold true in Boston as well.
  2. What types of housing units are being built in Boston over the years? Is luxury housing truly outstripping the production of affordable housing? I'm interested in this question after reading the Boston Globe article, which focuses on the Towers of Wealth luxury residences being built by the city, while affordable housing has not kept in quantity.
  3. How do luxury and non-luxury houses compare in terms of cash sales, current ownership, flipping, and investors? Who is buying luxury housing? From the Boston Globe article, it is clear that most Boston households cannot afford luxury residences - which poses the question: who is affording them? Is it mainly investors?

Discoveries & Insights

Summary

In this exploratory data analysis, we explore trends about flipping, cash sales, investors, current residence and more across different types of housing. We first check the data quality and find that there are definitely some errors in the dataset, with many residences priced at $0 and lot sizes above 50K sq ft. We then dive deeper into the questions we ask, and find that on average, luxury homes have a higher rate of investors buying them, cash sales, and flipping. Additionally, most of the luxury homes are concentrated in the downtown Boston area with varying flip terms.
